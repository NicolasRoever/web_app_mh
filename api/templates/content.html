Managing Stress and Supporting Your Mental Health

Managing stress is essential for maintaining your mental health. The World Health Organization's Doing What Matters in Times of Stress provides practical skills that help you stay grounded and resilient when life gets overwhelming.

Grounding Techniques

  • Deep breathing: Inhale slowly through your nose and exhale through your mouth to calm your body.
  • 5-4-3-2-1 exercise: Engage your senses by naming five things you can see, four you can touch, three you can hear, two you can smell and one you can taste.
  • Mindful movement: Take a short walk or gently press your feet into the floor to reconnect with the present moment.

Healthy Habits for Stress Management

  • Stick to a sleep routine: Aim for seven or more hours per night and keep consistent bedtimes.
  • Exercise regularly: Enjoyable movement releases tension and boosts mood.
  • Eat balanced meals: Nourishing foods and adequate water help your body cope.
  • Make time to unwind: Relax with hobbies, music or time in nature.
  • Connect with others: Supportive relationships buffer against stress.
  • Practice gratitude: Noticing positives each day encourages a balanced perspective.
  • Set boundaries: Prioritize tasks and say no when you need to.
